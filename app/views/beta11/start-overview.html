1. How it works

You can apply for a Budgeting Loan to help pay for:

  • furniture or household items such as white goods
  • clothing or footwear
  • rent in advance
  • costs linked to moving house
  • maintenance, security or improvements to your home
  • travelling costs within the UK
  • costs linked to getting a new job
  • maternity costs
  • funeral costs
  • repaying hire purchase loans
  • repaying loans taken for the above items

The smallest amount you can borrow is £100. The loan is usually paid into your account, eg a bank account.

If you get Universal Credit, apply for a budgeting advance from your local Jobcentre Plus instead.

Paying back the loan

A Budgeting Loan is interest-free so you only pay back what you borrow. The loan repayments are based on how much benefit you get and what you can afford.

The repayments will automatically be taken from your benefits. A guideline for what you might pay back is shown below:
